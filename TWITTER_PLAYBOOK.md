## 1. Voice & Positioning

### 1.1 Voice spec (one paragraph)

Dry, confident, slightly nostalgic. Speaks like a builder who already knows the answer to the question and is mildly amused that you're asking. Sentences end. No emoji except the four sanctioned glyphs (see §1.4). Reference Million Dollar Homepage explicitly and often — it is not a comparison, it is the lineage. Avoid hedging adverbs ("just," "simply," "kinda"), avoid hype intensifiers ("massive," "insane," "absolutely cooked"), avoid the entire NFT vocabulary. Founder talks in first person. Brand account talks in third person and only when there is something on-chain to point at.

### 1.2 Voice that IS

| # | Example phrase | Why |
|---|----------------|-----|
| 1 | *"20,736 pixels. One canvas. Forever."* | Three sentence fragments. No verbs. Factual. Final. |
| 2 | *"This is the Million Dollar Homepage with a price curve."* | Lineage + the one mechanic that matters. No selling. |
| 3 | *"Block (66, 37). Owned by [wallet]. Listed at 0.0009 SOL. The auction drops every block."* | Reads like a price tape, not a pitch. |
| 4 | *"I built this because nobody was going to."* | Founder, first person, unembarrassed. |
| 5 | *"The canvas filled 8% in 24 hours. The next 8% will be harder."* | Public-by-default founder math, no spin. |

### 1.3 Voice that ISN'T

| # | Forbidden phrase | Why |
|---|------------------|-----|
| 1 | *"gm fam, big things cooking"* | Every retired ICO grifter wrote this in 2024. |
| 2 | *"We are revolutionizing on-chain attention."* | Verb "revolutionizing," noun "attention," disqualifying combo. |
| 3 | *"Mint while supplies last anon!!"* | "Anon," double exclamation, urgency-scarcity sales tone. |
| 4 | *"Web3 advertising is dead. Long live Solana Billboard."* | The word "Web3" appears. Disqualified. |
| 5 | *"Massive shoutout to the goats over at @bigwallet for their alpha leak"* | "Massive," "goats," "alpha," sycophant tone. Also begs to be screencapped. |

### 1.4 Vocabulary lists

**Forbidden** (zero occurrences across founder + brand accounts during launch window):

`gm`, `gn`, `wagmi`, `ngmi`, `wen`, `ser`, `frens`, `anon` (as address), `alpha`, `alpha leak`, `bullish`, `bearish`, `cope`, `seethe`, `Web3`, `Web 3.0`, `Web3 advertising`, `decentralized advertising`, `attention economy` (as a phrase — "attention" alone is fine), `revolutionary`, `revolutionize`, `disrupt`, `disrupting`, `paradigm`, `mint while supplies last`, `last chance`, `LFG`, `to the moon`, `wen moon`, `dyor`, `1000x`, `100x`, `gem`, `hidden gem`, `apes`, `ape in`, `degen` (as identity claim — fine in irony), `whitelist`, `OG`, `based`, `cooked`, `not financial advice`, `this is the way`, `gigabrain`, emoji rockets, emoji gems, emoji fire (except in §1.5 sanctioned moments).

**Preferred**:

`canvas`, `pixels`, `region`, `block`, `the curve`, `the auction`, `the floor` (as price floor), `permanent`, `on-chain`, `fair launch`, `provenance`, `lineage`, `homage`, `rebuilt`, `2005` (the year MDH happened, drop it casually), `genesis region`, `the grid`, `coordinates`, `(x, y)`, `the treasury` (always linked), `Solscan`, `MPL Core`, `Dutch auction`, `bonding curve`.

### 1.5 Sanctioned glyphs (max one per tweet)

`🪩` (the disco ball, our unofficial mark — already in the share-button copy), `🟦` (block, only when literally pointing at a region), `📌` (pin, for milestone tweets), `🧵` (thread indicator). No others. No fire, no rockets, no money bags, no eyes, no skull.

### 1.6 Founder vs brand account split

| Account | Tweets | Retweets | Stays silent when |
|---------|--------|----------|--------------------|
| **Founder (@darko or whatever Darko confirms)** | First-person narrative, opinions, replies, the launch tweet, all reply-guying, the T-10 thread, AMAs, crisis acknowledgements | RTs the brand account's milestones (sparingly — max 1 RT per day from brand) | Pure brand promo with no commentary. The founder doesn't RT his own brand account's region spotlights — he writes the spotlight himself. |
| **Brand (@solanabillboard)** | State of the Canvas, milestone counters, draw mechanics, takedown notices, Solscan links, *factual* only | RTs the founder when the founder is making a factual claim (numbers, on-chain events). Never RTs founder opinions. | Anything emotional. Anything subjective. Any disagreement. The brand account does not have feelings. It has a treasury balance. |

**Rule of thumb**: if a tweet reads as "a person made a choice," it goes on the founder account. If it reads as "the system updated state," it goes on the brand account. When in doubt, founder.

**RT cadence**: Brand RTs founder ≤2x/day. Founder RTs brand ≤1x/day. Cross-RTs cluster around milestones, not as ambient amplification.

> **7/** Pricing has two zones. The center (60×34) is flat: 0.0005 SOL per block. The rest follows a bonding curve from 0.00004 to 0.0005 SOL per block as the canvas fills. The first buyer pays ~12x less than the last. That is on purpose.
>
> **8/** [IMAGE — 2005 MDH screenshot] This is what it looked like the first time. The first time, it cost a million dollars to fill. This time, it costs about six SOL. The economics are not the point. The artifact is.

**Hard rules**:

- Three stats only. Pct, wallets, current curve price. Not "boost revenue," not "listings." Those go in the weekly retro.

### 5.2 Region Spotlight (1-3x per week, founder account, 16:00 UTC)

A spotlight is *noticing*, not shilling. The difference:

| Shilling | Noticing |

| Tags the owner aggressively, asks for RT | Mentions the owner once if they have a handle, otherwise lets the wallet stand |
| Lists features ("animated GIF, premium location, boosted") | Describes what looking at it feels like |
| Ends with a CTA | Ends mid-thought, like the founder noticed it on the way to do something else |

### 5.4 How a region qualifies

A region gets a spotlight if **at least 2** of these are true:

1. The image is competently designed (legible at canvas zoom, intentional palette).
2. The link goes somewhere that adds context (not a 404, not a generic landing).
3. The region tells a small story by itself (composition, in-joke, weird specificity).
4. The owner is a community member who shipped early (first 100 wallets) — gratitude bias is allowed.
5. It is so bad it's good (memetic template only).

**Disqualifiers**: NSFW, IP infringement, scam destination, wallet that owns >5 spotlights already (rotate the limelight).

## 7. Crisis Playbook

Every scenario: pre-drafted response, decision tree, and timing.

### 7.1 Bot snipes the center zone in first 60 seconds

**Detection**: Mint pulse >50/min in T+1:00–T+1:01, all from <5 wallets, all in center zone.

**Response window**: ≤15 min.

**Tweet (founder)**:
```
Heads up — a wallet just minted {N} center-zone blocks in the first 60 seconds.

That's allowed. The center zone is flat-priced and first-come except for the 20 partner allocations we disclosed at T-1.

The wallet is [solscan link]. If they sit on the regions, they're stuck holding them. If they list at fair prices on the Dutch auction, that's the resale market doing what it should.

This is exactly why we pre-allocated 30% of the center zone to partners with public commitments. The other 70% is the open market. Both rules were public before launch.
```

**Do not**: blacklist, reverse, apologize. The mitigation is in the disclosure.

### 7.2 NSFW content uploaded to a region

**Detection**: Mod flag, user report, or founder spot-check.

**Response window**: ≤2h to takedown, ≤24h to public note.

**Action sequence**:
1. Hide the rendered image via the kill-switch (growth playbook §5.6 admin override).
2. Replace with a 1-pixel placeholder + "image hidden by moderation" tooltip.
3. The on-chain NFT is **not** burned, modified, or censored. Only the rendered display.

**Tweet (brand)**:
```
A region image was hidden today via our moderation flow ({date}, region (x,y)).

The image violated the content policy ({short reason}). The on-chain NFT is unchanged — we only control what the canonical site renders. The owner was notified.

Policy: solanabillboard.space/policy. Flag flow: in any region's sidebar.
```

### 7.3 Famous person's logo gets squatted

**Detection**: DMCA-style email, public callout, or founder spot.

**Response window**: ≤4h public response, ≤24h takedown.

**Tweet (brand)**:
```
Takedown executed on region (x,y) — uploaded image used a registered trademark of {entity} without authorization.

Image is hidden. NFT remains on-chain — the owner can re-upload a non-infringing image at any time.

If you own a trademark and find it on a region, the takedown form is in the footer of solanabillboard.space.
```

**Do not**: name and shame the squatter. The response is procedural, the framing is "we moved fast."

### 7.4 Twitter account gets ratio'd on the launch tweet

**Definition**: replies > likes by 2x within 4h.

**Do**:
- Read every reply. Sort by sentiment.
- Identify the *single specific* objection driving the ratio (price too high? bot snipe? NFT skepticism? Alex Tew weighed in negatively?).
- Reply once, in the same thread, with a substantive answer to the objection. From the founder account, not the brand.

**Don't**: delete the launch tweet. Don't subtweet the critics. Don't lock replies. Don't post a screenshot of "I'm being attacked."

**Recovery tweet template (founder, posted as a *reply* to the launch tweet, not as a new tweet)**:
```
Reading the replies. The most consistent pushback is {specific objection}.

Direct answer: {substantive 2-sentence response that does not equivocate}.

If that's not satisfying, the codebase is open and the on-chain state is public — both are linked in my bio. Disagreement is fine. Pretending I didn't see it isn't.
```

**Then**: new tweet (not a reply) 4h later about something else entirely (a region spotlight, a canvas screenshot). Carry on. Ratios decay if you don't feed them.

### 7.5 Whale buys 1000+ blocks at curve start

**Detection**: single wallet >1,000 blocks within first 6h.

**Reframe (founder, within 2h)**:
```
A wallet just minted {N} blocks across the curve zone in one transaction. That's about {pct}% of the canvas, and they paid the early-curve price for all of it.

Two ways to read this:
- Concentration risk: one wallet owns a chunk of the canvas.
- Conviction signal: somebody looked at the curve math and decided it was undervalued at the start.

Both are true. The Dutch auction floor exists for exactly this case — if they over-bought, the resale market sorts it. If they under-bought, they have the strongest expression of belief in this thing on-chain.

I'd rather have a wallet that bought 1000 blocks at the floor than 1000 wallets that bought 1.
```

The key reframe move: don't apologize for the concentration, name it as a stress test of the resale mechanic.

### 7.6 Alex Tew reacts negatively

**Scenarios**:

| What he does | Action |
|--------------|--------|
| Replies "Please don't use the MDH name" | Strip every direct MDH reference from active comms within 2h. New positioning: *"a fair-launch pixel canvas on Solana."* Reply to him publicly: *"Heard. The MDH framing is gone from our official comms — pinned thread updated. The lineage is obvious to anyone who was around in 2005, but we won't make it our headline."* Do NOT delete past tweets. |
| Tweets a critique without naming us | Don't engage. Subtweets are not invitations. |
| Tweets a critique naming us | Reply once, founder account: *"Read your post. Specifics: {1-sentence response per critique}. The codebase is open if you want to look at the actual mechanic."* No defensiveness, no thanks-for-the-feedback. |

**Tone rule**: never punch down at the originator. He has the moral high ground on naming, full stop.

### 7.7 Competing project launches a copycat in week 2

**Likelihood**: high. Solana has a 7-day clone cycle on anything that works.

Never *react* to the copycat in the same tweet that mentions us — the algorithm reads the co-occurrence and starts ranking us together. Keep them in separate tweets, separate days.

## 8. Don't-Do List (Twitter-Specific, Solana 2026)

The launch plan §6 listed: don't talk about "Web3 advertising," don't chase memecoin trends, don't engagement-bait. Adding 7 more:

1. **Don't tweet through the bonding-curve math.** A founder explaining the curve in three threads in launch week looks insecure about it. The T-4 thread covers it. After that, link, don't re-explain.
2. **Don't post a CT-screenshot ("look how Crypto Twitter is reacting!") of yourself.** It's the most-clowned move on Solana. If we're getting traction, the traction is visible. We don't need to narrate it.
3. **Don't tweet through the night chasing US wakeup.** Quality > frequency. The 12-hour gap during Asia (T+10 → T+22) is a feature — it gives the canvas time to fill and the next-morning tweet has new numbers.
4. **Don't reply to every QT in launch week.** Replying to a QT signals you read it; not replying signals confidence. QT replies are for substantive ones only (questions, partnership offers, smart pushback). Skip the "love this!" QTs.
5. **Don't post the founder picture.** The founder's identity is "the person building this alone." A face shot collapses the mystique. Keep it text-and-canvas. Headshot can ship in week 4 retro thread, not before.
6. **Don't use Spaces as the headline format.** Solana Spaces are saturated and the audience is the same 200 accounts every time. Use Spaces only as ambient signal, never as the lead distribution channel. The lead is always tweets + canvas screenshots.
7. **Don't quote-tweet partners' RTs of the launch tweet to amplify.** The QT-of-RT is the most desperate-coded move on the platform. Like their RT, reply with thanks if you must, but never QT it.
